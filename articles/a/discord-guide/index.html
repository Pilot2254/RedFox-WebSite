Welcome to the RedFox Community!

Last updated on 2024-09-21

Introduction to the Servers:

When you first join our Discord servers, things might seem a little overwhelming, especially if you're new to Discord or just don’t use it often. Don’t worry, we’ve got you covered! Let us guide you through the process, starting with onboarding and explaining the differences between RedFox Studios (RFS) and Team RedFox (TRF).

When you join our servers, the first step is to complete the onboarding process. This ensures you get the right roles and access to the channels that suit your interests. During onboarding, you'll have the chance to choose specific roles that best describe you, ensuring you’re placed in the right communities. After that, you’ll need to accept the server rules. Only then will you be able to interact and view the server's full content.

Difference between RedFox Studios and Team RedFox

RedFox Studios (RFS): This server is focused on all things development—whether it's programming, game design, 3D modeling, or any other creative pursuit. You'll also find announcements and updates about ongoing projects, events, and news from the world of development.

Team RedFox (TRF): This server is primarily a gaming community and esports team hub. Here, you'll find discussions around competitive gaming, favorite games, and esports events. Whether you're a casual player or an esports enthusiast, this is the place for you!

Onboarding at RedFox Studios (RFS)

Onboarding at RFS is simple and helps to tailor your experience to your interests:

  1. Select Your Gender: Choose between male or female. This helps us provide a personalized experience.
  2. Select Your Interests: Are you a programmer, designer, or 3D modeler? Choose what you are passionate about so we can connect you to the right channels and community.
  3. Select Notifications: Decide which types of notifications you want to receive. Options include:

Selecting Announcements and Updates is highly recommended to stay informed about community events and important information.

Onboarding at Team RedFox (TRF)

Onboarding at TRF is similar to RFS but geared toward gaming preferences:

  1. Select Your Gender: Choose between male or female. This helps us provide a personalized experience.
  2. Choose Your Favorite Games: Instead of selecting hobbies, you’ll select the games you play most frequently. Whether you're into CS2, Apex Legends, Rainbow Six Siege, or other popular titles, this helps tailor your experience and connect you with fellow players.
  3. Select Notifications: Like RFS, choose what kind of updates you want to receive. You’ll be able to stay on top of important announcements or esports-related news.
  4. Once this is done, you’re all set to jump into gaming discussions, find teammates, and explore!

Discord Rules

Both servers follow the same set of rules, which are designed to foster a respectful and fun environment. You can view the full rules at any time here. Please make sure you abide by these rules and, of course, the official Discord Terms of Service.

Basic Rules Overview:

  • Keep it respectful—light humor is fine, but bullying will not be tolerated.
  • No account sharing, boosting, or sharing private information.
  • Keep the server safe for work—no NSFW content.
  • Follow the guidelines for your username and avatar.
  • Respect the moderators and use the proper support channels.

For more details, check the complete list through the link above.

Discord Channels Breakdown

We have several types of channels available to streamline communication and ensure everyone can find what they’re looking for. Here’s a quick rundown of what you’ll see on both servers:

Channel Types:

  • Announcements: Official news, updates, and events related to the server.
  • Forum Channels: Used for support, feedback, and specific requests. If you encounter any issues or have suggestions, these are the channels to visit.
  • Chat Channels: The place to socialize, connect with others, and talk about gaming, development, or anything else relevant to the server's focus.
  • Voice Channels: Jump into voice channels to talk with friends or teammates. Please be mindful of voice channel etiquette, as we want to keep these spaces pleasant for everyone.

Some channels are restricted to moderators or staff and won’t be accessible to regular members. If you find a channel that you can't post in, it’s likely reserved for staff communication.

Support Channels

Need help with something? Want to leave feedback or report a bug? We’ve made it easy for you with Support Forum Channels on both TRF and RFS servers. In these channels, you can create a forum post outlining your issue or request. Whether you need technical support, community assistance, or have a suggestion, we’re here to help! Just be sure to check out the pinned posts or announcements in the forum to see if your question has already been answered before posting.
